The Calibrated Life

Finding focus at the intersection of nature and narrative.

The Marksman

Precision under pressure. The shooting range requires the same calibrated focus as a complex technical document. It’s an exercise in breath, mechanics, and unwavering concentration.

The Angler

The salt marshes of the Texas coast are where I go to decompress. Fishing is a skill born of patience and a deep respect for the unpredictable rhythms of the Gulf.

The Bibliophile

I find inspiration in Hemingway's "one true sentence" approach. My love for literature isn't just about reading—it's about understanding the blueprint of the human experience.

The Writer

Beyond the technical manuals lies a deep-seated need to arrange words until they bleed truth. Influenced by the spare, rhythmic prose of the 20th-century greats, I view writing as an act of stripping away the unnecessary to find the bone-deep narrative underneath.
